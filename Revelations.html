River water and glass has a peculiar taste. You squeeze your mouth shut, trying to keep as much of the dirtied water and grit from your lungs as you can. You still can't help but let a scream a echo in your starved lungs. Your brother is in the driver seat, eyes shut, his crop of blond hair tugged upwards as the car dives lower and lower. A cloud of blood - his own blood - chases him downward into the river. Funny, it looks a little bit like cigaratte smoke, the way it whispers up into the air. He hated how you smoked and said it'd kill you. Now you're fighting against your seatbelt, desperate for air and desperate for your brother to wake up. Panic rising in your heart, a fist unseen pounds against your ribcage - you can hear the panicked beat of your heart in your ears like war tribal drums, deafening, fast, getting faster - your vision clouds with little black stars as your fingers press down on the seatbelt release. His limp body drifts free. His eyes remain shut, peaceful, like he was just taking a nap. You scream at him to wake up, but the river swallows your voice, and all the adrenaline in the world can't replenish the air in your lungs or give you more time. There is no more time. You shove your body out the passenger side window and grasp at open water, pining for the surface, counting seconds as if they were your last. How long can you hold your breath? You look down, back at your brother's car - it fades away from you, like an object passing deeper into space. 1 seconds 2 seconds 3 seconds 4 seconds And air.
